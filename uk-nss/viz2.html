Questionnaire Responses by University (Bar Chart)



This is likely the viz with the most amount of information to process as it contains the percentage of students that agreed with every question displayed in pages across every university. The percentage of students that agreed with a question is calculated as the sum of the percentage of students who answered 4 (“moderately agree) and the percentage of students who answered 5 (“strongly agree”). The universities are sorted in descending order by their overall mean score so, while the bars are not strictly in descending order for each page, the universities remain in order and do not shift, which allows for easy comparisons. The colors are on a relative red-green divergent scale and represent the relative performances of each university on each question compared to the overall highest and lowest scores. Many questions generate feedback that skews either positive or negative across all universities which is why Question 1, for example, is comprised entirely of bars shaded green, even with many darker (better) bars towards the bottom, but questions like Question 25 are comprised almost wholly of red bars. It is possible to view the different pages (each corresponding to one question) using the interactive Tableau JavaScript embedded here.
