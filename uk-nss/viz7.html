Subject Satisfaction



This viz shows a treemap, similar to that in Viz 5, that breaks down overall student satisfaction by subject. It is color-coded as a sort of heat map on a red-blue divergent scale with blue representing the strongest performers and red the weakest. The subjects are also ordered by the satisfaction rating, defined here as the average mean score in response to Question 27 on the NSS: “Overall, I am satisfied with the quality of the course.” The colors have also been broken down into a stepped scale, with 5 values on the range that bin 4-5 subjects into each step. This allows us to easily compare tiers of subject performance while the order of the treemap allows for strict rankings upon closer examination. This viz presents some of the same information as in Viz 1, but it has a unique way of displaying it that provides different insights than its counterpart. We see that newer subjects (Computing, Design, and creative and performing arts) tend to perform lower than more established subjects (Historical, philosophical and religious studies, Physical sciences) which is perhaps attributable to time giving teachers more time and feedback to refine course content and learn how to teach it better. However, all subjects scored above a 3.7, indicating general satisfaction across disciplines.
