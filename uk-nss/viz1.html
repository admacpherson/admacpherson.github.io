Response Rate & Satisfaction By Subject



This bubble map shows the response and overall satisfaction rates for each subject across all UK universities. Satisfaction is measured on the 1-5 NSS scale in response to the final question: “Overall, I am satisfied with the quality of the course.” The colors are scaled on a relative basis, meaning the lowest subject has the darkest red (and vice versa) rather than a set benchmark (such as 1) being the dark red. This was done so that the subjects can more easily be compared to one another whilst having an absolute color scale would be best for comparing the overall quality of the subjects. The lowest score belongs to the relatively new subject of Computing with students giving it an average satisfaction score of 3.7463 while the highest is Historical, philosophical, and religious (HPR) studies with an average satisfaction score of 4.0823. This is likely due to the amount of time these courses have been around; Computing is new and so lecturers are still refining their teaching techniques while HPR Studies have been around for quite a bit of time by their very nature and likely have more refined teaching practices. However, this theory does not hold up across all subjects as Law, a relatively old subject, scored on the lower end of the scale with an average satisfaction score of 3.8133. All together though the courses demonstrate an overall satisfaction with UK higher education. The NSS defines a score of 3 as neither agreeing nor disagreeing and a 4 as mostly agreeing. Thus, even the lowest subject score tilts positive while the highest is right at the “Mostly agree” benchmark. The bubbles are also scaled by the response rate of students in each practice which is displayed as a decimal below each of the programme names.
