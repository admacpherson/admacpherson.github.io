Satisfaction at University of Roehampton



This viz, known as a treemap, provides an easy, yet complex, look at a specific set of answers to the NSS questions. The viz is color-coded on a relative red-green divergent scale where the worst-performing metric is a dark red and the best performing is dark green. This allows for quick comparisons of how feedback for each question compares to one another but is limited in how much it captures true feedback. Question 25, for example, has approximately a 51% agreement rate but is coded a dark red suggesting it performed quite poorly despite having most students expressing positive feedback. However, if it were shaded a lighter green, it would be more difficult to tell how it compares to other questions at a glance, even if we were given the impression of more positive overall feedback. The treemap does a good job not only at expressing quickly ascertainable comparisons but also at expressing the full text of the questions. Often, when looking at more linear charts it is difficult to comprehend or even see the whole of the questions but the treemap provides ample room to see them as well as visualize them. The viz also moves sequentially from positive to negative in a left-to-right, up-then-down direction which makes it naturally readable and thus comprehensible.
