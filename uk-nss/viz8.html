University vs Subject Heatmap



This is a heatmap of the mean NSS scores by university and subject, displayed as a table. The score is calculated as an average of all the answers across all the questions. The scale is relative red-blue diverging with 2.721 being the lowest score and 4.71 being the highest. The blanks on the table indicate that a university doesn’t teach a particular subject. Veterinary sciences, for example, is only taught at a few universities and thus its column is only filled for a few entries. The grid nature of the viz allows for at-a-glance comparisons by both subject and university. Reading across the table, one can read how a university generally performs (ex. lots of blues for one, lots of orange for another) and compare that to any other university. Reading vertically allows subject comparisons in the same manner and can provide a general idea of how well a subject performs on the NSS, both on its own and compared to other subjects. The table also shows the prevalence of subjects at universities; a relatively blank column for example, such as the aforementioned veterinary sciences, represents a subject not taught in many places while a particularly full column, such as business, represents a near-ubiquitous subject.
