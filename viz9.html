Mean Questionnaire Score by Subject



Viz 9, similar to Viz 8, looks at the mean scores, calculated as the average of all questions on the NSS, by subject and plots this metric as a dot for each university. By perusing the dots, one can see where each university ranks relative to the others on each subject. The dots are also color-coded by the average positive feedback rate, which is likely to mirror the mean score closely but can certainly differ. The colors are on a fixed absolute scale (rather than the relative scale used by other visualisations), meaning it looks at the percentage of students who responded on a scale from 0% to 100% instead of comparing the lowest score to the highest. This can be used to analyze how universities perform on the whole across the UK rather than how they compare to each other. The large swaths of green indicate that the state of UK education is very good, with the red dots being mostly outliers and certainly a minority to the green masses. While there are some tails behind many of the subjects, the only dot dramatically tailing its peers is Edinburgh Napier University’s veterinary science programme, which only had an average score of 1.6 (1 being the worst) and only 8% of answers being comprised of positive feedback. The dots are also scaled by the average response rate; though this is difficult to distinguish at first glance, it helps to highlight programmes with higher response rates slightly more due to a higher quality of data.
