Questionnaire Responses by University (Box & Whisker Plot)

Note: It is possible to mouse over the questions to view their full text



This box and whisker plot shows the distribution of responses to each question on the NSS across all universities. Together they show how UK universities perform overall on individual questions. Tighter distributions represent a more consistent performance, such as in Question 1, while wider distributions show a much more varied performance, such as Question 10. Though it is somewhat difficult to see when further away, the dots are also color-coded by median score on a relative red-green divergent scale with the worst universities being the darkest red and the best being the darkest green. On average, lower-scoring universities necessarily ought to be on the lower half of the box and whisker plots (and vice versa) but the color-coding allows outliers to stand out to identify what a particular university is doing exceptionally well or poorly. There aren’t a lot of examples of this, indicating that when a university does well it tends to do well across categories (and vice versa) rather than standing out only in some. However, one dot that does stand out is the lowest performer on Question 12 (“I have been able to contact staff when I needed to”). St. George’s Medical Hospital School is a very middle of the road school which results in its grey color (that makes it easy to miss) but it indisputably has the lowest performance concerning students’ ability to contact staff, a category that most universities did fairly well in. Even with the outlier of St. George’s, it still sits comfortably above the median of many other questions, indicating that perhaps the staff of St. George’s may want to improve their performance relative to other universities, but on the whole UK students are satisfied in this matter.
